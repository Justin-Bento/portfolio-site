Dr. Pressure is a pressure washing company based in Nanaimo, British Columbia, servicing communities across Vancouver Island, including Nanaimo, Campbell River, Courtenay, Comox, Duncan, Salt Spring Island, Victoria, and Ladysmith. The company specialises in residential and commercial exterior cleaning services such as driveways, home exteriors, patios, decks, and moss removal.

I worked remotely as a solo Website Designer and Developer for Dr. Pressure from July 2025 to October 2025. The engagement concluded after I completed my deliverables and client communication ended. This case study reflects both the design and development work delivered, as well as my experience working directly with clients as an independent website developer.

The project focused on three primary goals:

1. **Simple content editing experience** — Provide the client with an intuitive, user-friendly interface for updating website content without requiring technical expertise.
2. **SEO-friendly and responsive design** — Ensure the website is optimised for search engines and functions seamlessly across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
3. **Dynamic content management** — Enable the client to easily add and manage testimonials, reviews, and other promotional content to support business growth.

Step 1 – Simple content editing experience

I spent time researhing templates and came across three templates, Data Nove, Screw Fast and Stone. After discussing with the client the theme the client went with was Stone. Astro Stone Template acted as an anchor that supported the site’s functional needs, and my job was to integrate Keystatic with Astro to create a simple, non-technical content editing experience. The system was configured so the client could manage all content without touching the source code, and my goal was to provide training to the client so they could maintain the site independently.

Step 2 – SEO-friendly and responsive design

I redesigned and organised visual assets to meet browser requirements, strengthen brand identity, and prepare the project for handoff. This stage had two challenges: client communication became limited, which concluded my ability to continue receiving feedback, and there was a lack of established brand assets and general guidelines to follow.

With limited client feedback and no established guidelines, I extracted the primary colour from the business card provided, converting it to HSL, and customised the template following common UI design patterns. I applied a 90-10 colour rule to create a stricter, more minimalist approach suitable for high-impact, modern interfaces. Typography was carefully reviewed for legibility, with sample words tested across devices to ensure readability. All of these small details required a significant amount of time.

Step 3 – Linking a Content Management System

Once the template was selected and the digital assets were organised, the next step was to update the source code and integrate everything to create a seamless experience. Reflection

While this project was a valuable learning experience from a business perspective, if I were to do it again, I would focus on improving client engagement to prevent communication gaps. Working with Astro and Keystatic was a valuable learning experience, it proved slow for client delivery. Next time, I would likely use a platform such as WordPress to better manage the content editing experience.This would include understanding the client’s technical comfort level, sharing technical information at the right time, and presenting updates in a way that fits their workflow without overwhelming them.
