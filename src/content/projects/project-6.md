The LifeLine Canada Foundation (TLC) is a registered non profit organization focused on mental health support, suicide prevention, and public awareness. The organization develops initiatives intended to support individuals in crisis as well as those affected by the loss of a loved one to suicide.

In this project, I worked as Lead Designer. My responsibility was to help define the product’s early direction and translate the organization’s mission into a usable application framework. Specifically, this involved research synthesis, feature exploration, information architecture, and the development of visual design concepts.

The project focused on the LifeLine App, a proposed national, free suicide prevention and awareness application. Its primary objective was to provide accessible resources, guidance, and self support tools for individuals experiencing crisis, grief, or emotional distress.

During the early stages of the project, I identified concerns related to the long term technical overhead required to design, build, maintain, and host a dedicated mobile application. Given the scope and sensitivity of the platform, as well as the ongoing resource demands, the project direction subsequently shifted. Rather than pursuing full implementation, the emphasis moved toward establishing a strong conceptual and design foundation.

Consequently, due to timeline and resource constraints, my scope was limited to designing the core framework of the app. The objective was to create a clear structural system and cohesive visual narrative that could be extended by future designers and developers. This work included defining key features, navigation patterns, layout systems, accessibility considerations, and an overall visual language aligned with the organization’s values.

As a result, the primary outcome of this work was a cohesive design foundation intended to reduce ambiguity for future contributors. Instead of delivering a finished product, the project produced a shared reference point: a visual and structural blueprint capable of supporting iteration, handoff, and long term continuity as the project progressed beyond my involvement.

As the project remains ongoing, I have continued to check in periodically with the organization’s leadership to remain informed about its status and progression. In reflecting on this experience, I also recognized that many of the projects I undertook were scoped at a senior level while I was still operating as a junior. This resulted in attempting to assume levels of authority, context, and responsibility that exceeded my formal role at the time.
