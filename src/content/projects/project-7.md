Early in my career, I struggled with imposter syndrome due to conflicting advice about what a “proper” portfolio should be. Over time, I realized there isn’t a single correct format — a portfolio’s purpose changes as your experience grows. Early on, clarity matters more than polish, and even a simple PDF can be enough to communicate skills and intent.

As my experience developed, my website evolved alongside it, with earlier versions preserved under subdomains. The current version reflects a clear shift in focus: positioning myself as a Website Designer and Developer specializing in UI and Front-End Development. To reflect this dual focus, I drew inspiration from Final Fantasy Dissidia 012, a game built around contrasting identities existing under a single banner... . I translated this concept into the interface through an interactive toggle that allows users to switch between designer and programmer states, reinforcing the idea of dual expertise through interaction.

From a development perspective, I heavy customized a template called Dante theme by Just Good UI, chosen for its strong performance, accessibility, and maintainability foundations. By leveraging Astro, Tailwind CSS, and optimized image handling, I was able to focus on design decisions and content structure rather than rebuilding standard patterns. This approach reflects my preference for stable, well-supported solutions that scale cleanly over time.

Ultimately, this version of my website reflects where I am now in my career: someone who empathizes with both designers and developers, bridges design intent with technical execution, and values maintainability and clarity over novelty. As my skills continue to grow, the site will evolve again, with each iteration serving as a deliberate snapshot of that moment.
