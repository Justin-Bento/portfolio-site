I was one course away from earning my diploma in Web Design and Development at Humber College—until a sudden roadblock in iOS programming left me at a crossroads. Here’s what happened, what I learned, and how I moved forward. I reached out for help—emails to instructors, searching for tutoring—but resources were scarce.

While Humber technically offered a retake option through a separate mobile development course, the timing and structure didn’t align with my graduation timeline. It was a frustrating gap in the system: one class shouldn’t derail an entire degree, but sometimes, logistics outweigh flexibility Rather than delay graduation indefinitely, I made the pragmatic choice to switch programs. It wasn’t the path I’d planned, but it taught me a valuable lesson about resilience and adaptability. Today, I use those skills daily in my career—proof that detours don’t have to mean dead ends.

**You might be wondering: What is The Odin Project?** It’s a curated, open-source curriculum that aggregates the best online tutorials, blogs, and courses—all in one place—to teach full-stack web development in your free time. I chose to continue with it because it bridges the gap for self-taught learners, offering a structured, high-quality roadmap to becoming a developer without the cost of traditional bootcamps.
